Abstract 
This project is a sophisticated and multi-functional mobile and web application that seamlessly 
integrates advanced nutrition tracking, intelligent workout programming, and holistic lifestyle 
management into a single unified platform. Developed using React Native for mobile, React for 
the web dashboard, and a scalable Node.js backend with MongoDB, the platform offers a 
vertically integrated digital ecosystem that connects all aspects of a user’s physical journey 
including nutrition, training, and recovery through intuitive and highly responsive interfaces. At 
the core of the platform lies the concept of Smart Plans, which serve as dynamic AI-adapting 
schedules combining the characteristics of MyFitnessPal for precision macro-tracking, a 
dedicated personal trainer for progressive strength programming, and a life coach for habit 
formation. Each Smart Plan allows users to generate or customize 14 to 90 day routines where 
meals and workouts are not just logged but proactively scheduled, while the systems Food 
Intelligence and Smart Fill technologies automate the tedious aspects of data entry. Additionally, 
the platform integrates a futuristic Command Center for administrators to curate the global 
exercise and food databases to ensure data integrity across the ecosystem. The systems flexible 
architecture enables a wide range of use cases including beginners seeking guided 14 day 
transformations, athletes requiring precise macro-cycle planning, and busy professionals needing 
efficient and automated daily structures. To further enhance user adherence and motivation, the 
platform includes a comprehensive Lifestyle module that synchronizes water intake, sleep 
quality, step counts, and mental well-being to provide a 360 degree view of health performance. 
In summary, this project is far more than just a calorie counter or a workout log, it is a powerful 
all-in-one platform designed to build, sustain, and elevate personal fitness through the seamless 
integration of data analytics, artificial intelligence, and user-centric design.

Chapter 1: Introduction and Context 
In recent years, daily life has become more demanding, and people increasingly rely on digital 
tools to manage their health and lifestyle. However, most existing health and fitness applications 
focus on only one aspect, such as calorie counting or workout tracking. This often forces users to 
depend on multiple applications at the same time, making it difficult to maintain consistency and 
follow a balanced routine that addresses both physical and lifestyle needs. 
=== PAGE 10 ===
To overcome this limitation, the CoreIQ platform was developed as a unified solution that brings 
together nutrition tracking, workout planning, and lifestyle monitoring in one system. The goal 
of CoreIQ is to simplify the way individuals manage their fitness by providing a single 
environment where all related activities can be planned and tracked efficiently. 
CoreIQ allows users to create and follow personalized daily plans that guide their meals, 
workouts, and recovery habits. Instead of treating these elements separately, the platform 
connects them within one structured flow, helping users stay organized and focused throughout 
the day. This approach encourages consistency and supports long-term progress by making 
health management more practical and accessible. 
The platform supports a wide range of features, including detailed meal logging with nutritional 
values, structured workout routines with progressive load tracking, and daily lifestyle monitoring 
such as hydration and sleep. Users can also track their overall progress and set long-term goals, 
all within a secure and well-organized system. 
To support these functionalities, CoreIQ was developed using modern web and mobile 
technologies. The backend was built using Node.js and Express to handle application logic and 
provide secure communication between components. MongoDB was used as the database 
system to store user data efficiently, including fitness records, meal logs, and personal 
information. 
On the frontend side, React.js was used to develop the web interface, providing a responsive and 
interactive user experience. The mobile application was developed using React Native with 
Expo, allowing the platform to run smoothly on both Android and iOS devices. Communication 
between the frontend and backend is handled through RESTful APIs, enabling real-time updates 
and synchronization across devices. 
Overall, CoreIQ was designed as an accessible and flexible platform that adapts to different 
devices and user needs. By combining multiple health-related functions into a single system, the 
platform aims to help users manage their fitness more effectively while maintaining simplicity, 
security, and ease of use.

Chapter 2: Literature Review 
This chapter reviews existing digital health and fitness platforms that support nutrition tracking, 
physical training, and lifestyle monitoring. The objective is to analyze how current solutions 
address these domains individually or collectively, identify limitations in existing approaches, 
and position the proposed CoreIQ platform within the broader context of digital health systems. 
The review highlights a general trend of functional fragmentation in current tools and discusses 
how integrated platforms attempt to overcome this limitation. 
2.1 Digital Health Platforms: From Fragmentation to Integration 
Nutrition-Focused Platforms 
MyFitnessPal is one of the most widely used nutrition tracking applications worldwide and 
provides access to a large user-generated food database. The platform allows users to log meals 
and monitor caloric and macronutrient intake with considerable detail. However, its support for 
structured physical training is limited, as exercise is typically represented only as estimated 
energy expenditure rather than as part of a progressive training program. As a result, users 
=== PAGE 14 ===
seeking structured workout planning often rely on additional applications, leading to fragmented 
data management and reduced analytical insight [1]. 
Workout-Centered Platforms 
Applications such as Strong and Heavy focus primarily on resistance training and workout 
logging. These platforms offer effective tools for recording sets, repetitions, and training volume, 
making them suitable for strength-focused users. Despite their strengths in workout tracking, 
they generally lack integrated nutrition management, requiring users to manage dietary planning 
separately. This separation limits the ability to analyze the relationship between training load and 
nutritional intake within a single system [2]. 
Traditional Coaching Approaches 
In the MENA region, and particularly in Lebanon, fitness and nutrition coaching frequently relies 
on manual or semi-digital methods such as messaging applications, static documents, and 
spreadsheets. While these approaches may offer personalization, they typically lack real-time 
interaction, automated adaptation, and systematic progress tracking. The absence of integrated 
data collection reduces their effectiveness for long-term monitoring and dynamic plan adjustment 
[3]. 
2.2 Integrated Platform Approach 
To address the limitations of fragmented fitness tools, the CoreIQ platform was developed as an 
integrated digital system that combines nutrition management, workout planning, and lifestyle 
monitoring within a unified structure. The platform introduces structured daily plans that 
organize meals, training sessions, and recovery metrics into a single workflow. This approach 
enables users to interact with multiple health-related components without switching between 
separate applications, supporting consistency and centralized data analysis. 
2.3 Platform Architecture and Technology Choices 
The CoreIQ platform adopts a modular architecture designed to support scalability, flexibility, 
and cross-platform synchronization. The backend layer is responsible for handling business 
logic, user authentication, and data exchange through structured APIs. A document-oriented 
=== PAGE 15 ===
database model was selected to accommodate complex and nested data structures such as daily 
plans, workout sessions, and nutritional logs. 
On the frontend, the platform provides both web-based and mobile interfaces to ensure 
accessibility across devices. This dual-interface approach allows users to perform detailed 
planning tasks on larger screens while maintaining the ability to log activities and monitor 
progress through mobile devices. A unified synchronization layer ensures data consistency 
between platforms in real time. 
 
2.4 Addressing Gaps in Existing Platforms 
An analysis of existing digital fitness solutions reveals several recurring limitations that reduce 
their effectiveness in supporting long-term adherence: 
• Limited functional integration: Most platforms specialize in either nutrition or 
training, requiring users to manually correlate data across multiple systems. 
• Static planning models: Many tools rely on fixed plans that do not adapt to 
schedule changes or performance variations. 
• Data accuracy concerns: Crowdsourced databases often contain inconsistent or 
inaccurate entries, affecting the reliability of nutritional analysis. 
• Minimal lifestyle context: Sleep quality, hydration, and mental well-being are 
frequently excluded despite their influence on physical performance. 
 
The CoreIQ platform was designed to address these gaps by integrating multiple health domains 
into a single system, supporting dynamic plan modification, and maintaining structured and 
curated data management.

Integrated fitness platforms are particularly valuable in real-world scenarios where users require

In practical use, a user may plan a short-term fitness program using a web interface and

automatically across platforms. This workflow illustrates the advantages of unified system 
design in supporting consistent engagement.

Chapter 3: Requirements Analysis 
 
This chapter defines the functional and non-functional requirements of the proposed platform. It 
outlines the system capabilities required to support integrated nutrition, training, and lifestyle 
management, as well as the quality attributes that ensure reliability, security, and usability. The 
chapter also identifies the primary user roles and describes how the system adapts to different 
usage contexts across web and mobile environments. 
 
3.1 Functional Requirements 
The platform is an integrated digital system accessible through both web and mobile 
applications. It supports two primary user roles: administrative users and standard users. Upon 
authentication, users are presented with a personalized dashboard that provides access to system 
functionalities based on their role and usage context. 
The core functional requirements of the system are as follows: 
• User Authentication and Access Control: 
The system shall provide a secure authentication mechanism that supports user 
registration, login, and session management across multiple devices. 
• Plan Generation and Management: 
The system shall enable users to generate structured fitness plans that combine nutrition 
targets and workout schedules. These plans shall support variable durations ranging from 
14 to 90 days and allow controlled modification throughout the program lifecycle. 
• Nutrition Tracking: 
The system shall allow users to record daily meals using a centralized food database. 
Nutritional information, including macronutrient values, shall be automatically calculated 
and aggregated for daily and long-term analysis. 
=== PAGE 18 ===
• Workout Tracking and Execution: 
The system shall support structured workout logging, including the recording of 
exercises, sets, repetitions, rest intervals, and training volume. Performance data shall be 
stored to support historical analysis and progression tracking. 
• Assisted Workout Logging: 
The system shall provide automated assistance mechanisms that suggest workout 
parameters based on previously recorded user performance, reducing manual input while 
maintaining accuracy. 
• Lifestyle Monitoring: 
The system shall support the tracking of lifestyle-related metrics such as sleep duration, 
hydration levels, daily step count, and self-reported well-being indicators. 
• User Profile and Goal Management: 
The system shall allow users to manage personal attributes such as age, height, and 
weight, as well as fitness objectives. Changes to these parameters shall trigger automatic 
recalculation of relevant system targets. 
• Administrative Data Management: 
The system shall provide administrative users with tools to manage and verify shared 
data resources, including food items and exercise definitions, ensuring consistency and 
accuracy across the platform. 
• Context-Aware User Interfaces: 
The system shall adapt its interface behavior based on the user’s device, prioritizing 
planning and data visualization on web platforms and activity logging on mobile devices. 
3.2 Non-Functional Requirements 
In addition to functional capabilities, the platform must satisfy several non-functional 
requirements to ensure performance, security, and overall system quality. 
• Scalability: 
The system shall be capable of supporting a growing number of users and data records 
=== PAGE 19 ===
without degradation in performance. The architecture shall allow for horizontal expansion 
to accommodate increased demand. 
• Performance: 
The system shall provide responsive interaction for core operations such as data retrieval, 
plan loading, and activity logging. Mobile functionality shall support temporary offline 
operation with deferred data synchronization. 
• Usability: 
The system shall be designed to support users with varying levels of technical and fitness 
experience. Interfaces shall prioritize clarity, consistency, and ease of navigation across 
devices. 
• Security and Privacy: 
The system shall enforce strict security measures to protect personal health data. This 
includes secure authentication mechanisms, encrypted data transmission, and role-based 
access control. User data shall remain private and accessible only to authorized entities. 
• Responsiveness and Synchronization: 
The system shall maintain consistent data synchronization between web and mobile 
platforms, ensuring that updates performed on one device are reflected across all 
connected environments. 
• Data Integrity: 
The system shall enforce validation and consistency checks on all critical operations, 
including nutritional calculations, plan progression, and activity logging, to prevent 
inaccurate or misleading data representations.

fitness, nutrition, and lifestyle activities. User interactions include profile configuration, plan

administrative capabilities, the Super Admin manages the administrative hierarchy itself. This 
includes creating and deactivating administrator accounts, assigning roles, and controlling access 
permissions. These responsibilities ensure controlled governance, operational stability, and 
secure system administration. 
The use case diagram reflects a role-based access model in which each actor interacts with a

3. Polymorphic References 
Several fields in the CoreIQ schema support polymorphic or flexible referencing, allowing a 
single collection to associate with multiple entity types or represent hierarchical/self-referencing 
relationships. 
● audit_logs (Polymorphic Actor Reference) 
The audit_logs collection uses a polymorphic reference to track actions performed by different 
actor types. 
• Fields involved: 
o actorType (Enum: user, admin, system) 
o actorId (ObjectId) 
This design allows a single audit log to reference either a User, an Admin, or a System-
generated action, without duplicating log structures across multiple collections. 
It provides flexibility for tracking security events, content moderation actions, and system 
operations within a unified logging mechanism. 
 
=== PAGE 44 ===
 
● admins (Self-Referencing Polymorphism) 
The admins collection supports a self-referencing relationship through the createdBy field. 
• Field involved: 
o createdBy (Ref → admins._id) 
This enables hierarchical administrative control, where a Super Admin can create and manage 
other Admin accounts. The relationship is polymorphic in nature because it allows an entity to 
reference another entity of the same collection while representing different roles. 
 
● plans (Hybrid System/User Creation Source) 
The plans collection includes a flexible creation model through the createdBy attribute. 
• Field involved: 
o createdBy (Enum: user, ai) 
This field distinguishes whether a plan was generated directly by the system wizard, modified 
through AI assistance, or manually created by the user. While not a reference-based 
polymorphism, it represents behavioral polymorphism at the application logic level. 
 
● ai_conversations (Embedded Polymorphic Messages) 
The ai_conversations collection stores chat messages using an embedded structure that supports 
multiple speaker roles. 
• Fields involved: 
o messages.role (Enum: user, assistant, system) 
This allows a single conversation document to contain messages originating from different 
logical actors, enabling flexible AI-human interaction without splitting data into multiple 
collections.

4 . Summary  
 
This ERD provides a comprehensive blueprint of the CoreIQ data structure and illustrates how 
ObjectId-based references, embedded documents, and polymorphic associations work together to 
support a scalable and flexible NoSQL architecture. The schema is designed to balance 
performance and maintainability by combining referenced relationships for high-volume data 
with embedded structures for tightly coupled information. 
 
Polymorphic elements such as actor-based audit logging, hierarchical administrative 
relationships, and role-based AI conversation records contribute to the extensibility of the system 
while preserving a clear and organized data model. These design choices allow the platform to 
evolve without requiring significant structural changes to the database. 
 
Although MongoDB does not enforce relational constraints at the database level, all relationships 
are strictly controlled within the application layer through Mongoose schemas, validation rules, 
and reference consistency checks. This approach ensures data integrity while retaining the 
flexibility advantages of a NoSQL system.

Chapter 4: System Design 
4.1 Introduction  
The CoreIQ platform is engineered as a sophisticated, AI-enhanced fitness and nutrition 
ecosystem designed to bridge the gap between rigid planning and adaptive lifestyle guidance. 
The system architecture utilizes a high-performance, cross-platform technical stack, featuring 
a React Native mobile application for user interaction and a React-based web dashboard for 
administrative oversight. These frontends communicate seamlessly with a 
scalable Node.js and Express backend, forming a robust API-driven architecture. 
Data persistence is managed by a MongoDB NoSQL database, chosen for its ability to 
efficiently store and query complex, nested data structures such as multi-week workouts plans 
and granular activity logs. The platform distinguishes itself through a "Hybrid Intelligence" 
model, integrating deterministic algorithms for plan generation with state-of-the-art Generative 
AI services (Google Generative AI / Groq) for contextual guidance and natural language 
modifications. The overall architecture adheres to the Model-View-Controller (MVC) pattern 
with a layered service approach, ensuring a clean separation of concerns, high maintainability, 
and the flexibility to scale independent microservices as the user base grows.

5.4 Backend: Web Services (Node.js + Express) 
The backend architecture is developed in Node.js with Express.js, utilizing Mongoose for 
ODM, implementing a layered Service-Controller pattern. 
 
System Architecture 
• Controllers: Handle HTTP requests (req/res), parse inputs, and return standardized 
JSON responses. 
• Services: Encapsulate pure business logic (e.g., Plan Generator, AIContextManager), 
keeping controllers lean. 
• Models: Strict Mongoose schemas for User, Plan, Meal, Workout, and AuditLog. 
=== PAGE 57 ===
• MiddleWare: Handles cross-cutting concerns (Auth validation, Error handling, Rate 
limiting). 
Authentication & Security 
• JWT Strategies: Stateless Bearer token validation for protecting API routes. 
• Bcrypt: Salted credential hashing for storing passwords securely. 
• Validation:  Middleware using express-validator to sanitize all incoming request bodies. 
• Email Security: Code-based verification flow via Brevo API (HTTPS) to bypass 
insecure SMTP ports. 
Access Control (RBAC) 
• MiddleWare Guards: protect (User valid), admin (Role checks), superAdmin (Elevated 
privilege). 
• Granular Scopes: Separation of “User Data” (Private) vs “Content Library” 
(Public/Shared). 
Database Design (MongoDB + Mongoose) 
• Hybrid Schema: references (ObjectId) for relations between Users/Plans, but Embedded 
Documents for daily logs inside Plans to optimize read performance. 
• Indexing: Strategic indices on frequently queried fields (username, email, planId). 
 
5.5 Backend: Mobile Services (API Layer) 
The backend exposes a RESTful API optimized for the specific data needs of the React Native 
client. 
Mobile-Centric APIs 
• Auth: POST /api/auth/* (Register, Login, Verify Email, Reset Password). 
• Plans: POST /api/plans/create (Wizard), GET /api/plans/active (Dashboard Sync). 
=== PAGE 58 ===
• Logs: POST /api/meals & POST /api/workouts (Automatic activity recording). 
• AI: POST /api/ai/chat (Context-aware conversation), POST /api/ai/edit-plan (Natural 
language logic). 
• Content: GET /api/foods/search, GET /api/workouts/filter. 
Business Logic & Data Flow Plan Generation: 
1. User submits goals (e.g., "Lose Weight, 4 days/week"). 
2. PlanService calculates TDEE and nutritional targets. 
3. Algorithm selects templates from Workout library matching the split. 
4. System generates a 14-Day schedule document and saves to MongoDB. 
5. Frontend receives the full object and renders the calendar view. 
AI Interaction: 
1. User sends "Swap lunch for tacos". 
2. Backend fetches current Active Plan + User Diet Preferences 
3. Constructs a dense Prompt with this context and Sends to Gemini. 
4. Gemini returns JSON instructions ({ action: "replace", meal: "lunch", items: [...] }). 
5. PlanController executes the database update and returns the new state. 
 
5.6 Summary: Data Flow and Integration 
The data flow of CoreIQ is designed for near-real-time synchronization between the "Smart 
System" and the user's daily actions. 
User-Facing Workflows 
• Activity Logging: When a user logs a meal, the app optimistically updates the local UI 
(Progress Rings) while asynchronously syncing with the MongoDB backend. 
=== PAGE 59 ===
• AI Assistance: The "Hybrid Intelligence" model ensures that while AI handles 
the reasoning (chat, advice), the core system handles the execution (database writes, 
math), ensuring data integrity is never hallucinated. 
Admin Operations 
• Content Loop: User submissions flow into a "Pending" collection. Admins review these 
via the Web Dashboard. upon Approval, items are atomically moved to the public "Global 
Library" collection, instantly becoming available to all mobile users via search. 
• Integrity Monitoring: Real-time aggregation pipelines provide the Admin with live stats 
on "Active Users Today" and "Total Workouts Completed," visualizing the heartbeat of 
the platform.

Chapter 7: General Conclusion and Future Recommendations 
 
In conclusion, this senior project, CoreIQ, successfully addresses the limitations of traditional 
fitness systems that rely on static plans and disconnected tracking tools. Instead of generic, one-
size-fits-all approaches, CoreIQ introduces an intelligent and adaptive platform designed to meet 
the evolving needs of modern athletes. By unifying nutrition tracking, workout planning, and 
performance monitoring within a single ecosystem, the platform provides users with a structured 
and data-driven approach to managing their health. 
 
CoreIQ functions as a personalized digital assistant for fitness management. Nutritional targets 
dynamically adapt based on daily activity, workout plans evolve according to user performance, 
and AI-driven insights help simplify complex health data into actionable guidance. This 
integrated approach reduces manual effort while improving accuracy and consistency across the 
user’s fitness journey. 
 
The project demonstrates how modern technologies can make advanced fitness coaching more 
accessible. Through real-time metabolic calculations, seamless synchronization between mobile 
and web platforms, and a clear, intuitive user interface, CoreIQ establishes a solid foundation for 
scalable digital health solutions. The system emphasizes user autonomy, precision, and long-term 
sustainability, enabling individuals to take informed control of their physical well-being with 
confidence.

7.1 Future Work 
While CoreIQ delivers a comprehensive and functional platform, several enhancements can 
further extend its capabilities and impact: 
1. Wearable Device Integration 
Integrating with platforms such as Apple Health, Google Fit, and Garmin would enable 
real-time synchronization of activity, sleep, and energy expenditure data. This would 
allow more precise automatic adjustments of caloric targets and recovery insights. 
2. AI Computer Vision for Nutrition and Training 
Incorporating computer vision could enable calorie estimation from food images and 
real-time exercise form analysis, reducing reliance on manual data entry and improving 
training safety. 
3. Trainer Marketplace and Professional Portal 
Expanding the administrative interface into a dedicated trainer portal would allow 
certified coaches to manage multiple clients, assign personalized plans, and monitor 
progress remotely. 
4. Social Features and Gamification 
Introducing challenges, leaderboards, and social sharing features could enhance 
motivation and accountability through community engagement. 
5. Offline Mode Support 
Implementing a local-first database architecture would ensure uninterrupted app usage in 
low-connectivity environments, with seamless data synchronization once a connection is 
restored. 
6. Advanced Micronutrient Tracking 
Extending the nutrition system to include vitamins, minerals, and hydration metrics 
would provide a more comprehensive view of overall nutritional health.

7. Multi-language Localization 
Adding internationalization support for multiple languages and regional measurement 
systems would improve accessibility and usability for a global audience.
